Property register

What is the property register?

The property register identifies the location of the property with a short description (usually the address) and by reference to an official plan.

It may also give details of any rights that benefit the land.

For a leasehold title, it will give brief details of the lease.

Proprietorship register

What is the proprietorship register?

The proprietorship register specifies the “quality” of the title and contains details of the legal owner(s).

It shows whether there are any restrictions on the owner’s ability to sell, mortgage or otherwise deal with the property.

It may hold details of the price paid for, or value of, the property.

Charges register

What is the charges register?

The charges register contains details of mortgages, and notices of any other financial burdens secured on the property. It does not disclose amounts of money involved.

It also contains details of other rights and interests to which the property is subject such as leases, rights of way or restrictions on the use of the property.
